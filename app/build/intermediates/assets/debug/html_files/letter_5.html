Habits

Habits start to became ingrained when they’re associated with a delightful follow-up emotion. But whether this emotion is pre-built into the habit or you create it for yourself, as soon as you complete a healthy habit, even a small one, you should immediately celebrate your success.

Stanford university professor and habit expert B.J. Fogg first popularised the celebration concept. It’s really very simple...

Celebrating immediately after completing a healthy habit creates a memory imprint in your mind. So over time you learn to associate the habit with the positive emotion.

Don’t worry if at first you have to create the positive emotion yourself — eventually it will become second nature.

Why is it so difficult to get rid of addictive habits like taking drugs or watching pornography? Because a positive, powerful feedback emotion kicks in immediately after the action. This feedback tells your brain it was great to do this habit— you felt happier, and it was really pleasant.

In other words, you’re getting rewarded for a habit regardless of whether it could be very bad for your health in the long run. So you start getting addicted, trying to revive this positive emotion again, and again.

We’re now going to reverse engineer this in order to reprogram your brain to feel great about healthy habits— even those that can be painful at first.

Exercising comes to mind first. When you start exercising regularly, it feels like a monster you need to conquer every morning. You feel pain.

But if you keep it up over a long period of time, you’ll come to enjoy those feelings of pain -they’ll become sources of pleasure and you’ll find yourself looking forward to your next exercise session.
