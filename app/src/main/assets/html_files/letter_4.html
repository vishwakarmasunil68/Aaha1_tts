Why you shouldn't add any more habits for now ?

Behavioral change is all about tiny habitual changes. This is why new year resolutions never work : “I’ll start by running tomorrow for 30 minutes, and do it for the whole week!”.

Our new year resolutions tend to be ignited by a wake-up call: a sneering smile by a colleague, an eyeful of belly fat in the mirror...

But it always goes like this:

You get that first surge of energy and take a firm resolve, but it soon falls apart. You lose that initial fiery intensity of change ignited. You either completely forget about your resolve or have it in mind constantly without acting on it.

We unfortunately fall into this trap all the time.

Just compare last year’s goals with this year's; you’ll find a disturbing similitude. Year after year, you seem to have the same goals: lose weight, start an exercise regimen, wake up earlier, etc.

"The man who views the world at 50 the same as he did at 20 has wasted 30 years of his life." said Muhammad Ali Clay, one of the greatest heavyweight boxers in sport history.
